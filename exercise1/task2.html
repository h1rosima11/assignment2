Nursultan Nazarbayev

About Nursultan Nazarbayev

Nursultan Nazarbayev (born 1940) is a Kazakh statesman who served as the first President of independent Kazakhstan from 1991 to 2019

Timeline

  1. 1940 — Born in Chemolgan, Almaty Region.
  2. 1989 — Becomes First Secretary of the Communist Party of Kazakhstan.
  3. 1991 — Leads Kazakhstan to independence; orders closure of the Semipalatinsk test site.
  4. 1997 — Decision to move the capital from Almaty to Astana (then Akmola).
  5. 2019 — Steps down as President of Kazakhstan.

Contributions

  • Nuclear disarmament and closure of the Semipalatinsk test site.
  • Capital development and national projects (Astana, EXPO 2017).
  • Market reforms and attracting foreign investment in energy and industry.
  • “Multi-vector” foreign policy and Eurasian economic integration initiatives.
